Data Modeling & DBMS Fundamentals

Build a strong foundation in representing real-world data and the database systems that keep it organized.

Chapter 1

Data Modeling

Create clear blueprints of the domain using entities, attributes, and relationships. Entity-relationship diagrams (ERDs) capture how business concepts connect, while keys and constraints keep instances unique and accurate.

Great models separate conceptual thinking from physical storage so the design can evolve without disrupting the business rules.

Chapter 2

DBMS Fundamentals

Database management systems execute SQL, optimize queries, and enforce ACID properties so data stays trustworthy. Indexes and storage engines speed up reads, while constraints prevent invalid values.

Understanding these building blocks helps you design schemas that perform well and are easy to maintain.
