Data Modeling & DBMS Fundamentals

Concept Summary

Model the real world with entities/attributes/relationships. Understand tables (relations), rows (records/tuples), columns (fields). Use keys (primary/foreign/candidate) and constraints to keep data accurate. DBMS beats file-based systems by reducing redundancy and improving consistency/security.

Mini Example

Metaphor: Library. Books (entity) with attributes (Title, ISBN). Members borrow books (relationship). Barcodes = keys.

Real: ERD → Book(ISBN PK, Title, AuthorID FK), Author(AuthorID PK, Name), Loan(LoanID PK, MemberID FK, ISBN FK).
