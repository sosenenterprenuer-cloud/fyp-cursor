Chapter: Data Modeling

Data Modeling

Create blueprints that capture the relationships between entities, attributes, and business rules. Clean models reduce redundancy and keep future enhancements manageable.

Use entity-relationship diagrams (ERDs) to visualize how information connects, and rely on keys and constraints to enforce accuracy.

Chapter: DBMS Fundamentals

DBMS Fundamentals

Database management systems execute SQL, optimize queries, and enforce ACID properties so data stays trustworthy. Indexes and storage engines speed up reads, while constraints prevent invalid values.

Understanding these building blocks helps you design schemas that perform well and are easy to maintain.
